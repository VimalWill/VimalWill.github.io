Deep Learning Architecture for Motor Imagery Signal Translation
Neural signals from the human brain are primarily gathered from the "Motor Cortex," a region responsible for coordinating muscular movements throughout the entire body. These collected signals undergo processing in the Fourier domain, after which they are translated into corresponding muscular activities based on predictions made by a neural network model.

Keywords: Neural Networks, TensorFlow, Signal Processing, Brain-Computer Interface

Reverser Engineering on MFCC Algorithm
The process involves reconstructing Mel-frequency cepstral coefficients (MFCC) for understanding the process flow. Window functions, such as Kaiser and Blackman, play a crucial role in mitigating energy leakage in the signal spectrum. By applying multiple windows, we observe variations in energy leakage, leading to improved signal processing. The results highlight that Kaiser and Blackman windows are effective, enabling better feature engineering by eliminating unwanted signal artifacts.

Keywords: Mel filter banks, Blackman window, Fourier Transforms
